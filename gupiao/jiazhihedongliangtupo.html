Amazon (AMZN) reported sales and profits well above estimates, with healthy performance across the main segments. Revenue guidance was below estimates, solely due to currency fluctuations assumptions. Amazon tends to sandbag guidance, so the guidance number is no problem.

The Amazon investment thesis is as strong as ever, and the stock is still attractively valued at current prices.

The Numbers

Total sales grew 13% to $143.3 billion in the first quarter. North America increased 12% to $86.3 billion. International segment sales increased 10% year-over-year to $31.9 billion. Amazon Web Services (AWS) segment sales increased 17% year-over-year to $25 billion.

Profit margins are expanding vigorously due to scale efficiencies in retail and logistics, contributions from advertising, and the positive margin impact of AWS. Free cash flow net of lease obligations expanded from a negative $4.45 billion to a much stronger $48.78 billion.

Amazon still has abundant room to optimize logistics and reduce its costs to serve customers, with scale and technology being crucial competitive strengths in retail. Advertising is still a young opportunity with promising potential in terms of both revenue growth and profitability.

The biggest positive in the report is the acceleration of the cloud business combined with massive profitability at scale from this segment. The charts from J Ball show that revenue growth at Amazon Web Services has accelerated materially, which confirms that the optimization headwinds are already over.

Looking at absolute revenue numbers for AWS, the trend is quite clear.

Now that we have data from Amazon, Microsoft (MSFT), and Google (GOOG) (GOOGL), we can look at the absolute revenue numbers of the big three together. There was a strong acceleration in growth during COVID, then a cyclical pullback as customers optimized their cloud spending, and growth rates are now back in line with the long-term trend.

Management explained in the conference call:

We're seeing a few trends right now. First, companies have largely completed the lion's share of their cost optimization and turned their attention to newer initiatives. Before the pandemic, companies were marching to modernize their infrastructure, moving from on-premises infrastructure to the cloud to save money, innovated at a more rapid rate, and to drive more developer productivity. The pandemic and uncertain economy that followed distracted from that momentum, but it's picking up again. Companies are pursuing this relatively low-hanging fruit in modernizing their infrastructure.

And with the broadest functionality by a fair bit, deepest partner ecosystem and strong security and operational performance, AWS continues to be their strong partner of choice. Our AWS customers are also quite excited about leveraging gen AI to change the customer experiences and businesses. We see considerable momentum on the AI front where we've accumulated a multibillion-dollar revenue run rate already.

Because of the booming demand for AI computing power, Amazon will increase capital expenditures in AWS over the next few quarters. This means that investors should be prepared for some volatility in free cash flow, but the opportunity is well worth it.

Notably, the operating margin at AWS reached 37.5% of revenue last quarter, so the company can afford to spend a few extra bucks building capacity for AI opportunities.
